Chinese bird-and-flower paintings, pagodas, and pavilions, even Chinese emperors and local figures became their sources of inspirations. 東方對西方的好奇 Eastern Interest in the West 十七、十八世紀，歐洲的工藝品經由貿易、餽贈等方式進入清代宮廷，引起皇帝極大的興趣。吸鼻煙成為新的流行，裝盛物從西洋鼻煙盒改成了鼻煙壺。西洋琺瑯及玻璃在宮廷作坊開發出新的顏色，西洋人物和風景也成了陶瓷上的裝飾題材。西洋鐘錶及帶有彩色寶石的配件成為帝后喜愛的飾品。各種精巧可愛的舶來品則被珍藏在多寶格。 想一想，你是用什麼方法認識世界的呢？ European art and crafts were brought into the Qing court in the 17th and 18th centuries. Some were diplomatic gifts while others were imported goods. Snuffing became a new trend but the containers went from snuffboxes to snuff bottles. Qing artisans developed new colors for enamel and glass, and they would paint Western scenes on the objects as decorations. Emperors and empresses enjoyed western clocks as well as other bejeweled accessories. Various lovely foreign objects were collected in the curio-boxes. How do you explore and learn about the world?

### 303
![IMG_4804](img/IMG_4804.jpg)
硯友圈 Inkstone Enthusiast Community 明清士人結社聚會，談詩論政，書畫交流，愛硯者則品硯、藏硯，於硯上書銘刻字，以硯相贈。文人或精於斫硯，工匠亦熟知書文，硯台與他們的仕宦生涯、人際交遊、生命經歷相交相纏。即使事過境遷，硯台依舊，刻記著過往的人事遞嬗。 During the Ming and Qing dynasties, literati would form social gatherings to discuss poetry, politics, and engage in calligraphy and painting exchanges. Those who had a fondness for inkstones would appreciate and collect them, inscribe words or dedications on the inkstone, and exchange these inkstones as gifts. Scholars skilled in carving inkstones would collaborate with artisans well-versed in literature, intertwining the inkstone with their scholarly careers, social connections, and life experiences. Even as times changed, the inkstone remained, bearing witness to the people and events of the past.

雙硯與色墨 Dual Inkstones and Colored Ink 批注公文或圈點書籍時，常用紅字，故雙硯應運而生。而色墨常用以寫經、作畫，或明清科舉閱卷及批閱重大刑案時，分別以藍、紫等各色，代表各職司的官員。 When annotating official documents or highlighting books, red ink is commonly used. As a result, dual inkstones came into existence. Colored ink, on the other hand, is often employed in writing scriptures, creating artwork, or during the Ming and Qing dynasties' imperial examination paper reading, as well as the reviewing of significant criminal cases. Different colors such as blue and purple were used to represent officials from various government departments. 墨有五色 The Five Colors of Ink 「運墨而五色具」，是指墨色蘊含濃、淡、乾、濕等的豐富變化，而最好的墨色是淡而不暗，黑而不滯，光亮而有神，其關鍵在於良墨，是現成墨汁所無法企及。 The rich variations in ink colors encompass qualities like thickness, lightness, dryness, and wetness. The best ink color is one that is light but not dull, black yet not stagnant, bright and lively. The key lies in high-quality ink, which cannot be achieved by simply using ready-made ink solutions.

### 303
![IMG_4813](img/IMG_4813.jpg)
早期硯式 Early Inkstone Styles 考古出土最早可以稱為「硯」的是戰國至漢代的板硯，多取自天然石塊，表面平整，打磨光滑，並附有研石。東漢流行動物蓋鈕的三足石硯，六朝隋唐墓經常出土多足的圓形硯，並成為宋元以後仿古硯的形制之一。 The earliest artifacts that can be called "inkstones" in archaeological discoveries are the slab inkstones from the Warring States Period to the Han Dynasty. They were mostly made from natural stones, with a smooth and polished surface, and equipped with a grinding stone. During the Eastern Han Dynasty, three-legged inkstones made of stone materials with animal-shaped lids gained popularity. In tombs from the Six Dynasties, Sui Dynasty, and Tang Dynasty, multi-legged round inkstones were constantly unearthed, and they later became one of the traditional forms of inkstones imitated during the Song and Yuan Dynasties.

玉質細滑，適合製硯嗎？ Is a Smooth Jade Suitable for Making Inkstones? 硯以易於發墨又不傷筆墨為佳，硯材宜軟硬適中、粗細恰當，玉質堅韌細緻，較適合研磨硃墨，或硯堂局部不打磨，以增加摩擦力。 For inkstones, the ideal qualities include easy ink release without damaging the brush or ink. The material of the inkstone should strike a balance between softness and hardness, and an appropriate level of coarseness. Jade, being both sturdy and delicate, is better suited for grinding cinnabar ink or for leaving certain parts of the inkstone unpolished to increase friction during use.

It was a commonly used material in early times, with the Chengni inkstones from Jiangzhou, Shanxi province being a representative example. ### 303
![IMG_4821](img/IMG_4821.jpg)
臺灣硯石 Taiwanese Inkstones 清嘉慶年間文獻記載，濁水溪一帶產溪石，可為硯。道光至同治年間（1848-1864）在臺灣任官的王衢，曾經採拾宜蘭蘭坑羅溪石，製成《露布硯》一方，石質灰黑，帶金屬礦砂，是現存臺灣十九世紀用硯的少數例證之一。 During the Jiaqing reign of the Qing Dynasty, historical records mentioned that stones from the Zhusui River area could be used for making inkstones. From the Daoguang to Tongzhi reigns (1848-1864), Wang Qu, an official stationed in Taiwan, collected stones from the Luo River in Lankeng, Yilan, and crafted the "Lu-bu Inkstone." These stones were greyish-black in color and contained metallic minerals. They stand as a rare example of 19th-century Taiwanese inkstones that have survived to the present day.

硯式的完備 The Maturation of Inkstone Styles 隨著宋代科舉制度的成熟，社會上形成龐大的士人群體，這群廣大的用硯人口為日後硯石的發展奠定了基礎。1987年以前台灣中、小學生都是用毛筆寫作文，常用長方形硯台磨墨，這種平底的硯式源於宋代硯底挖空的「抄手硯」，您想知道他們是如何演變的嗎？ With the maturity of the imperial examination system during the Song Dynasty, a large group of scholars emerged in society. This extensive population of literati laid the foundation for the development of inkstones in the future. Prior to 1987, students in primary and secondary schools in Taiwan used brushes to write compositions and often used rectangular inkstones to grind ink. This flat-bottomed inkstone style originated from the handle inkstone of the Song Dynasty, which featured a hollowed-out base. Would you like to know how those inkstones evolved?

### 303

風字硯 Inkstone in the Shape of Chinese Character "Feng (wind)" 硯底有足，硯尾抬高，方便墨汁向後溜流至硯首，墨池和硯堂沒有明顯的區隔。硯尾寬，硯首窄，硯面俯視為梯形，其前身為箕形硯。 The inkstone has feet at its base, and the tail of the inkstone is raised, making it convenient for the ink to flow backward towards the inkstone's head. There is no distinct separation between the inkwell and the inkstone platform. The tail of the inkstone is wide, while the head is narrow. When viewed from above, the inkstone's surface appears trapezoidal. The predecessor of this inkstone was the winnower-shaped inkstone.

### 303
![IMG_4825](img/IMG_4825.jpg)
抄手硯 Handle Inkstone 抄手硯是宋代硯式的主流，硯底挖空，墨池陡降，硯邊明顯，硯形平整，其前身為風字硯。明代抄手硯的硯面平整，硯底常刻意以圓柱保留端石特有的石眼。 The handle inkstone was a popular style of inkstone during the Song dynasty. It is characterized by a hollowed-out base, a steeply sloping ink reservoir, prominent edges, and a flat shape. Its predecessor was the inkstone in the shape of Chinese character "Feng (wind)". During the Ming dynasty, the handle inkstone had a smooth surface, and the base was often intentionally carved into a cylindrical shape to preserve the distinctive stone-eye feature.

### 303
![IMG_4829](img/IMG_4829.jpg)
長方形硯 Rectangular Inkstone 硯底無足亦無側牆，重視硯面墨池與硯堂的各種形式，如鳳池、括囊、井田硯等，並且賦予各種意涵，甚至不分墨池與硯堂，呈現硯石本身自然的紋理，是明清硯的主流之一。 The rectangular inkstone is characterized by a base without feet or side walls. It features various forms of the ink surface, ink reservoir, and inkstone platform, such as the Phoenix pond (feng ci), enclosing pouch (gua nang), well-field inkstone (jing tian), and so forth. Each form carries different meanings and even blurs the distinction between the ink reservoir and the inkstone platform. This style showcases the natural texture of the inkstone itself and was one of the mainstream inkstone styles during the Ming and Qing Dynasties.

硯與研 Yan (硯) and Yan (研) 早期「硯」稱「研」，最初硯的主要功能是和研石併用，將天然礦物顏料研磨成粉末。隨著人工墨的出現以及逐漸演變為手可執握的墨條，以書寫為主的硯才成為主流。 In the early days, Yan (硯) was referred to as Yan (研). With the emergence of manufactured ink and the gradual evolution into handheld ink sticks, inkstones used for writing became the mainstream.
