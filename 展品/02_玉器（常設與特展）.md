各種服飾用玉 春秋、戰國之交，由於服飾的改變，束腰的帶鉤開始流行。造形、紋飾變化多端。漢代時更流行以盤蟠為造形的帶環。帶扣則是受到鮮卑服飾影響下的帶頭。 「韘」(she)本是射箭時套於大拇指上的皮套，目的是避免拉弓弦時割傷拇指。商、西周時就偶見用美玉雕琢的「韘」，又稱為「射決」。使用時要在內爾填塞織物。春秋時玉韘逐漸普及，增加了彎鉤。戰國開始，玉韘逐漸脫離實用性，朝向扁平化、裝飾化發展，當時文獻多稱之為「玦」。 「鉛卯」、「勝形佩」是流行於東漢，具有辟邪意義的佩飾。後者又名「司南佩」。 Ornamental Jades At the turn of the Spring and Autumn Period and the Warring States Period, belt hooks in various styles and patterns gained popularity because of changes in the way people dressed. Later during Han dynasty belt loops in the shape of coiling chi tiger were fashionable while under the influence of the ethnic Xianbei costume buckles also came into use. She was a protective leather gear worn on the thumb to avoid bowstring cuts during archery. There were occasionally jade thumb rings from as early as Shang and Western Zhou dynasties, also known as shejue, which had to be stuffed with fabric before use. The use of jade shes became popular during the Spring and Autumn Period, added with a curved hook. Then they went flat and ornamental, gradually removed from practical use, commonly referred to as jue in the period writings. Cubic gangmao amulets or sheng-shaped pei ornaments, both worn as pendants, were in vogue during Eastern Han dynasty, the latter also called sinan.

### 玉器-喪葬玉
![IMG_4620](img/IMG_4620.jpg)
喪葬玉 「喪葬玉」一詞指各類專為亡者製作，以強化亡者「精氣」，助其達於「再生」或「不朽」境界的各類玉器，包括： 一、西周晚期開始流行蓋住亡者面孔的玉面飾。 二、漢代時除玉眼蓋外，更用短柱形塞，塞住鼻、耳等人體孔竅。 三、春秋時期開始流行在亡者衣服上縫綴玉片，稱為「鱗施」。 四、漢代時最高級的葬服是用金、銀、銅、絲等綴縷，綴連玉片組成全套「玉匣」，將亡者包裹密實，只有頭頂部位縫綴的玉璧中孔，可供靈魂通過。 五、戰國時從楚文化區開始，流行用碧玉製作各式玉璧及成對玉珩、龍形佩，用於隨葬，西漢時有了「玉匣」制度後，在本組內用絲帛綑綁多件玉璧於亡者前胸後背，玉珩、龍形佩仍放在棺內。 六、較為一般的葬俗，是在亡者口中、手心，填以各式「哈」、「握」。到漢代時，流行含玉蟬，握玉豬，祈求亡者「再生」，家族能「繁衍興旺」。 Funerary Jades Funerary jades were specifically made to help fortify the deceased's jingqi (vital force) in the journey reaching next life or eternity, including: 1. Jade face covering, popular born in the 9th century. 2. Jade eye covers as well as jade plugs to seal off nostrils and ears etc., used in Han dynasty. 3. Jade slices sewn to burial garb, called scales, popular during the 8th to 3rd centuries. 4. Burial garb of the topmost grade in Han dynasty was a jade casing sewn of jade squares with gold, silver, copper, or silk threads, tightly enclosing the deceased inside. The only opening is a hole in the center of the jade bi disc sewn at the head end of the casing, for the soul to pass through. 5. During the Warring States Period, a new trend started from the Zhu Culture in the south which used dark green jade to fashion burial ornaments such as bi discs in various styles, pairs of heng pendants or dragon-shaped pei pendants. Then in Han dynasty, along with the custom of jade casings, bi discs were also tied to the chest and back of the deceased while heng and pei ornaments were placed inside the casket. 6. A more common practice was to put a jade piece inside the mouth and in each of the hands, the former called han and the latter wo. Jade cicadas and jade pigs were used as mouthpieces and palm pieces, respectively, during Han dynasty, wishing for the deceased to be reborn and the family to prosper.

![IMG_4637](img/IMG_4637.jpg)
追慕三代的仿古玉器 宋代理學深度結合了釋、道的教義與思想，然其本質仍是儒學，因此除關注自然的運作，更在意人世的秩序，故追求合理的禮儀規範，仰慕三代。宋儒從歸納古器物形制、判讀銘文、比對歷史制度，官方與私人並出版圖錄，以更正國家舊日祭典用器的謬誤，落實對儒家經典的理解與實踐。復古風尚遂被帶動，古穆風格成為宋代美學的重要特質之一，對此後的玉器發展，影響甚深。 Jades in Antiquarian Style Modeled on Ancient Times New Confucianism in Song incorporating the teachings of Buddhism and Taoism paid great attention to both the operations of nature and the order of the human world. Accordingly they sought rational ethics and etiquettes as guidance and admired exemplary models set by ancient dynasties. Song Confucian scholars categorized ancient vessels, elucidated inscriptions, compared historical institutions, and published private or government catalogues to rectify mistakes in contemporary protocols of national ritual systems and vessels, all for the purpose of fulfilling the theory as well as practice of the ideals as taught in the Confucian Classics.

During Eastern Zhou dynasty, states such as Qi and Lu practiced the five-element system preached by the metaphysical school of Ying and Yang. After unification of the land, centralized regimes of Qin and Han further exploited the concept, having its followers advocate that all ancient sage-kings when bestowed with the mandate of heaven would perform the ritual ceremony of Fong Shan in order to stress the orthodoxy, or legitimacy of the rule.

### 玉器展廳（玉之巧 單元）
![IMG_4675](img/IMG_4675.jpg)
The Ingenuity of Jade Carvings 西元1368年至1945年 1368 to 1945 C.E. 明朝可謂是歷史上最複雜的時代，一方是極端保守的極權政治，一方是因商品經濟萌芽而鬆動的社會階級，反映於文化藝術上，則是異常多變，甚至矛盾對立的風格並存。在「技也，而能近乎道矣」的觀念下，工匠受文人的拔助，而有類似現在「自創品牌」的情形出現，得到前所未有的重視。 中國玉器發展至清，由於高宗乾隆皇帝愛玉，又在二十五年（1760）征服回疆後，和闐美玉大量而穩定的輸入中原，主客觀因素的圓滿配合，玉器製作遂達到空前繁榮的景況。然而亦在此時，出現市場時尚與帝王品味背馳的情況，雅與俗、復古與時新的衝突，反而造成時代風格更形豐富、有趣。 玉器工藝在乾隆朝攀越巔峰後，逐漸進入平緩中醞釀變化的時代。最大的轉折在於，中國七千年來對玉質的認定產生改變，翡翠以其光豔四射，有別於閃玉溫潤內斂的美感，取代了閃玉，成為近、現代人們珍愛的玉種。 「玉之巧」單元分四項子題。首先是檢討「粗大明」觀念的正確與否，以進一步瞭解明代玉雕風格的多樣性。再則羅列以「御製」、「御用」款或御製詩的玉器，讓作品自身呈現清朝皇室用玉的磅礴氣勢。然後，觀察乾隆皇帝與當時玉器風格形成的關係。最後，從清晚期的玉器理解現代一般大眾對玉喜愛的類型與特色。

### 玉器展廳（寫實與辟邪 / 動物形玉雕）
![IMG_4681](img/IMG_4681.jpg)
寫實與辟邪 商晚期至西周，用閃玉製作的動物形立雕，多為體積不大的厚片或圓柱、方柱體。東周時，鐵工具漸普及，提升了琢玉技術。不但作品體積增大，動物的頭、尾、肢體也不再侷限於固定的塊體內，有了動作與神情。有的較為寫實，也有的增添了象徵神性的冠、角、翼、牙等，以為能辟邪致福。小件立雕常帶有穿孔，可穿繩繫掛。 Realism & Mysticism The animal-shape nephrite carvings from late Shang to Western Zhou dynasties were generally small and came in blocks, cylinders or cuboids. As the spread of iron tools during the Eastern Zhou period helped upgrade the skills of jade artisans, their works grew in size and no longer confined the subject animal's head, tail, and body into one single space; each part started to exhibit individual postures and expressions. Some of these animal carvings were more realistic and others were endowed with crowns, horns, wings, or fangs symbolic of divine power for blessing and bixie (warding off evils). Smaller works in the round often came with string holes for wearing or hanging.

### 玉之德（神主與鳩杖）
![IMG_4719](img/IMG_4719.jpg)
「神主」與「鳩杖」 「神主」就是神祖牌位。從石家河文化晚期到西周早期（約西元前2000-977年），先民用美玉製作或雕有面紋、或素色的「神主」，後者上面有時寫有祖先名號。商族屬東夷族系，將雕有玄鳥紋飾的玉飾插嵌於長杆上，祭典中用以依附自天而降的神祖之靈。發展到漢代，流行象徵王庇佑眾生的「鳩杖」。 Ancestral Tablet & Turtledove Staff Shenzhu (ancestral tablet) is a plaque symbolizing a specific ancestor. From the late Shijiahe Culture to early Western Zhou dynasty (c. 2000-977 B.C.E.), people made jade tablets which were either carved with patterns or plain but inscribed with the deceased ancestor's name. The Shang people, of Yi clan in the east, in ritual ceremonies used long wood poles top-set with jade ornaments of black bird designs for ancestral spirits to descend upon. During Han dynasty such poles developed into staffs topped with a turtledove, signifying the imperial benevolent protection for all his subjects.

### 玉之德（圭璧制度）
![IMG_4722](img/IMG_4722.jpg)
「圭璧制度」的確立 西周時，高級貴族墓葬中常將大玉戈疊在玉璧上，構成一組「圭璧」，放在墓主胸前或棺蓋上，所用之戈、璧，不乏前代古物。東周以降，大玉戈逐漸演變成無中脊、邊刃，也無刃部、柄部之分的尖首圭。除可與玉璧成組使用外，周、漢之時也曾與斜刃者組成所謂「圭璋」。 The Establishment of the Gui-Bi Ritual During Western Zhou dynasty, deceased high aristocrats were often buried with a large jade ge dagger place over a jade bi disc. The set, very often antiques from even earlier periods, was placed either upon his chest or the cover of his coffin. During Eastern Zhou dynasty, such large daggers gradually evolved into gui tablets with a double-bevel tip, with neither spine nor side edges, and without clear demarcation of the blade section from the handle. In addition to forming a Gui-and-Bi set, gui was also used during the 8th to 2nd centuries B.C.E. in combination with single-bevel-blade tablets as a Gui-and-Zhang set.

### 玉器展廳-玉之德/玉璧區
![IMG_4728](img/IMG_4728.jpg)
法力無邊的玉璧 玉璧是禮制地位最高的玉器。祭祀之外，還用於招賢納聘及元旦朝賀等儀式中。喪禮中玉璧用以裹尸，綴連在全套玉匣包裹在人頭頂的部位，或釘於棺材頭端，希望引領魂靈通過玉璧中孔，達到「太一」之境。戰國至漢代玉璧上的紋飾有：雲紋、勾連雲紋、穀紋、蒲紋、乳丁紋等。有的還雕琢龍鳳紋，四神紋與吉祥語。 The All-Powerful Jade Bi Disc The Jade bi disc was the top article in the ritual hierarchy. It was used in sacred worship as well as secular ceremonies, such as in recruiting talent, proposing a marriage, betrothal gifts, new year audience and celebrations at the court, and so on. A funerary suit sewn of jade pieces would wrap the deceased body, with a bi disc specifically for the apex of the head or the head end of the coffin, so the soul would hopefully pass through the central hole of the disc into eternity. The patterns that adorn the jade discs from the Warring States Period to Han dynasty include designs in cloud, cloud scroll, grain, rush mat, embossment, and sometimes with motifs of dragon-phoenix or four-deity, and auspicious phrases.

### 玉器展廳-石英家族區
![IMG_4752](img/IMG_4752.jpg)
五彩繽紛的石英家族 The Colorful Quartz Family 自然界中最容易被發現的美石是石英家族。石英的成分是二氧化矽，又依其晶粒是否能在顯微鏡下被看到，而分為顯晶質與隱晶質（或稱：粗晶與微晶）兩類，可分別稱作水晶與瑪瑙。玉髓因為帶有各種致色色素與內含物等因素，會呈現不同的顏色與外觀。玉髓若帶有條紋、紋狀色斑，就是國人熟悉的瑪瑙。中國境內蘊藏的瑪瑙多又分佈甚廣，距今約五千多年，長江下游的北陰陽營文化、凌家灘文化中，頗多用玉髓、瑪瑙製作的玦飾、璜等裝飾品，甚至還用瑪瑙製作斧頭。在西周時，突然大量出現全紅色的玉髓珠、管，到也玉髓、閃玉等串連成美麗的組珮，本院這類藏品展於「玉之德」單元。 文獻記載，自古就從西域傳入各種瑪瑙，所以，很難從瑪瑙的色澤文理推測其產地。通常色帶平行直線的稱「縞瑪瑙」，紋理細密層層纏繞的稱為「纏絲瑪瑙」，似綠繞瑪瑙就稱作「苔蘚瑪瑙」和「柏枝瑪瑙」。玉髓若含有銅、鎳、鐵等致色色素會呈現藍色、綠色與黃色。前二種本院藏品中沒有見到。院藏名品「三連章」曾被訂名為「黃玉」，距今多年前經比重測定，改名為「黃玉髓」；近日再測得以折射指數與拉曼光譜，更確定其質地為玉髓。完全不透明的玉髓稱為碧石，本院有名的肉形石就是顏色呈帶狀的碧石。 透明無色的水晶中，若含有三價鐵離子就會呈現黃色或紫色。若含有鈦離子，就會呈現粉紅色。若含有鋁離子，且受到微量輻射，破壞了透明水晶的原子結構，就會變成煙水晶（或稱為茶晶），如果太濃就成了墨晶。至於本院有名的髮晶雕馬背猴，就是水晶內包裹了金紅石或電氣石的針狀晶體。內部裂璺等的粉晶、紫晶、煙晶等，在清宮中常被稱作「碧霞」。此間第1至4櫃展出五彩繽紛的石英家族，第5櫃將粉晶、紫晶並列。至於帳冊舊名為「綠水晶」者，近日經測折射指數與拉曼光譜，確定為與祖母綠同一家族的海藍寶石，故與嵌有祖母綠的水晶罐併陳於第9櫃。

### 玉器展廳-石英家族區
![IMG_4753](img/IMG_4753.jpg)
The Quartz family boasts beautiful stones that can be most easily found in nature. Mainly consisting in silica, texturally it can be either phaneritic (macrocrystalline) or aphanitic (microcrystalline), and commonly categorized as rock crystal and chalcedony, respectively. Various colorants and inclusions also give quartz different hues and appearances, among which agate is a familiar type of chalcedony featuring colors in bands or masses. China is rich in chalcedony deposits, which are found in many areas across the country. The Beiyinyangying Culture and Linjiatan Culture, both in the lower reach of Yangzi River and dated more than 5000 years ago, had many jue earrings and huang ornaments made of chalcedony. There were even agate fu axes. Then in Western Zhou dynasty, all-red chalcedony beads and tubes suddenly appeared in large quantity; they were threaded with other types of chalcedony as well as nephrite to compose beautiful sets of pei ornaments in pendant style, featuring in the "Virtue of Jade" section of the exhibition. Literature records show that agate had been imported from Western Regions to China since the remote past. It is difficult to infer the place of origin for a given piece of agate based on its colors and textures. There are several types of agate: onyx, parallel striped; sardonyx, tightly entwined; moss agate, with chlorite inclusions; dentritic agate, with pyrolusite inclusions. Chalcedony with colorant contents such as copper, nickel or iron would appear blue, green, and yellow. The former two are not seen in the Museum collection, whereas the renowned Tri-Seal set was once named as yellow "jade" and have since reclassified as yellow "chalcedony" after a density test, confirmed again recently using the refractive index and Raman spectroscopy. Chalcedony of total opacity is called jasper; the famous meat-shaped stone is of a jasper with banded colors. Transparent, colorless rock crystals would become tinted by the presence of different ions: ferric iron, yellow or violet; titanium, pink. With aluminum ions, and if exposed to trace radiation altering the crystalline structure, the color would change to grayish brown, called smoky quartz (or brown quartz), or even to darker shades under higher concentration. The well-known carving of monkey riding horse in the Museum collection is a rock crystal of acicular prism included with rutile or tourmaline. Rose quartz, amethyst, and smoky quartz with many inner crack lines were often called "tourmaline" in the Qing court. The cabinets 1 to 4 show the specimens from the colorful quartz family, and cabinet 5 juxtaposes a rose quartz and a tourmaline for comparison. So-called "green crystal" as recorded in the old palace catalogues has been reclassified by the refractive index and Raman spectroscopy as aquamarine, belonging in the same category as emerald. Therefore it is placed in the same cabinet (9) with the emerald-inlaid crystal jar.

陸子剛以「工緻倣古」的特色，受文人雅士的稱頌。方志、筆記中記載他用昆吾刀刻玉，但歸納實物，子剛仍是以解玉砂琢磨玉器的形、紋，只是落款以刀刻。其風格不僅在當時蔚為流行，還出現大量的冒名之作，甚至到二十世紀仍不斷仿造。 Lu Zigang was best known for his "fine workmanship and archaic style" and for this feature much admired by the literati class. Local chronicles and literati notes said he worked his jades with the legendary kunwu knife. Judging from the actual works of his, however, we know that the forms and patterns were done by the conventional method, jieyusha, and only his signature was carved out by knife work. The popularity of Style Zigang extended beyond its time and imitations or fakes abounded, well into the 20th century.
