The design of an inkstone in the shape of a "yu gui" carries the meaning of pursuing a career in government service or officialdom. 井田硯 井田是指將田地畫分成井字形的方塊，士人以筆墨為生，將硯稱為「硯田」，以筆耕田，或戲謔自比為耕田的牛。 Well-field Inkstone Well-field Inkstone refers to an inkstone design that is reminiscent of the layout of land distribution in a checkerboard pattern. Scholars who made their living through writing likened their inkstone to their "tian," meaning fields. This metaphor highlights the significance of their writing tools in their intellectual endeavors.

### 硯展（硯形分類）
![IMG_4848](img/IMG_4848.jpg)
魚式硯 水中悠遊的魚兒，具有「如魚得水」的美好寓意，清代取自東北龍興之地的魚化石又另有一層飲水思源、永不忘祖的警惕之意。 Fish-shaped Inkstone Fish-shaped Inkstone refers to an inkstone design inspired by the graceful movements of fish swimming in water. The imagery of fish in water carries the beautiful connotation of being in one's element, which means feeling comfortable and at ease. During the Qing Dynasty, the design of fish-shaped inkstones drew from fossilized fish found in the homeland of Manchurian. This design also carries the metaphorical significance of "drinking water and remembering its source," serving as a reminder to never forget one's roots and heritage.

蕉葉、枯木與竹的造形硯 「雨打芭蕉」與「蕉葉題詩」是文學的浪漫，枯木老幹是擷取自大自然的強勁生命力，竹子則具有許多美好的意涵。竹筍可作佳餚，竹林清雅怡人，而像徵君子德性的竹節，更成為製硯發揮的重點，無論縱觀或橫視之，均可賞、可用。 Inkstones in shapes of Banana Leaf, Withered Wood and Bamboo "Raindrops on Banana Leaves" and "Inscribing Poems on Banana Leaves" are romantic literary expressions. "Withered Wood" draws from the strength of nature's resilience, portraying the tenacity of life even in its mature stage. "Bamboo" symbolizes lots of goodness. Bamboo shoots are enjoyed as delicacies, bamboo groves create a tranquil atmosphere. The symbolism of the Bamboo nodes, representing the virtue of a noble person, becomes a key element in crafting inkstones. Regardless of the perspective, these designs are both visually appealing and functional.

They would create rubbings and write inkstone catalogues to document their inkstones. Apart from this, scholars would gather with fellow inkstone enthusiasts to appreciate and talk about inkstones, forming a community of inkstone experts. ### 硯展（與硯為友 / 文化記憶）
![IMG_4851](img/IMG_4851.jpg)
文化記憶 Cultural Memory 硯石堅固，猶如碑石，可歷代相傳，形成文化記憶的載體。硯上文化名人的刻銘，引發今月曾經照古人之嘆，而以蘭亭修禊與羲之換鵝旳故事為題材，或是表現蘇軾雋永的文學傳奇，更是硯石最愛傳誦的內涵。 Inkstones, with their durable nature akin to stone tablets, can be passed down through generations and become carriers of cultural memory. The engraved inscriptions of cultural figures on inkstones evoke admiration and reflection on the past. Themes such as the Orchid Pavilion Gathering and Writing Scriptures for Geese serve as inspirations while portraying the timeless literary legends of Su Shi.

### 硯展（硯的珍藏）
![IMG_4852](img/IMG_4852.jpg)
硯的珍藏 Preserving the Inkstone 硯不可一日不洗滌，洗硯除了去垢，還要以清水養石潤。為了避免燥石，收藏硯的硯盒不宜用金屬材質，大多為漆盒或木質，量身訂製。再者，凡經手、過目的佳硯，沒有相機也無妨，繪圖、模拓，加上文字描述，留下永恆的記錄。 An inkstone should never go unwashed for a day. Cleaning the inkstone with clear water not only removes impurities but also nourishes the stone. To prevent the inkstone from drying out, it is advisable to store it in a dedicated inkstone box made of materials such as lacquer or wood rather than metal. These boxes are often custom-made to fit the inkstone precisely. Furthermore, for cherished inkstones that have been appreciated and examined, much have been passed down even without a camera. Drawing, making rubbings, and adding written descriptions can create enduring records, preserving their beauty and significance for an eternity.

### 硯台特展
![IMG_4861](img/IMG_4861.jpg)
呂留良（1629-1683）浙江海寧人，明代淮莊王女婿王煐的後代，家境富足，自幼喜愛硯石，與黃宗羲、吳之振等人為硯友，以「友硯堂」為堂號。 Lyu Liuliang (1629-1683), a native of Haining in Zhejiang, was a descendant of Lyu Ying, the son-in-law of Huaizhuangwang during the Ming Dynasty. Coming from an affluent family, he had a deep passion for inkstones from a young age. He shared his enthusiasm with fellow inkstone aficionados such as Huang Zongxi and Wu Zhizhen. He adopted the hall name "You Yan Tang", which translates to "Hall of Inkstone Friends."

### 硯台特展
![IMG_4862](img/IMG_4862.jpg)
林佶、余甸、黃任和林涪雲等人是清代康熙年間福州地方附近的愛硯人。他們有人藏書豐富，又或精於賞硯、擅長硯銘，還有篆刻家、刻字名家等。因常將佳石交付浙江斫硯名家顧二娘，往來密切，形成藏硯圈。 Lin Ji, Yu Dian, Huang Ren, and Lin Fuyun were inkstone enthusiasts from the vicinity of Fuzhou during the Kangxi reign of the Qing Dynasty. Some of them possessed extensive book collections, excelled in appreciating inkstones, were skilled in deciphering inscriptions on them, and included seal engravers and renowned engravers among their ranks. They often entrusted fine stones to the accomplished Fuzhou inkstone carver Gu Erniang in Zhejiang, maintaining a close connection and forming a community of inkstone collectors.

鳳池硯 蘇軾曾將福建建州的鳳凰山產石（一說為南平的黯黮灘石），作硯至美，命名為鳳味，後人援引蘇軾的說法，將硯面設計為鳳形，稱為鳳池硯。 Inkstone with Phoenix-shaped Pool Fengchi Inkstone refers to an inkstone design inspired by the imagery of a phoenix. Later, people followed Su Shi's description and designed inkstones in the shape of a phoenix, which came to be known as "feng chi yan", or "Fengchi Inkstone."
