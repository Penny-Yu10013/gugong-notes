### 神獸想像／西洋想像
![IMG_4270](img/IMG_4270.jpg)
獅子 Lion 「獅子」古稱「狻猊」是源自印度的發音，最早隨著佛教傳進來中國。雖然早就有獅子傳入中國的記載，但因為數量稀少，到了明代也僅限皇家園囿中眷養，藝術家們鮮少看過真正的獅子，對於獅子產生了許多不同的想像。雖然畫作中獅子的大小接近實物，但仍有一些差距。牠們雄偉氣派的形象，成為重要機關出入口的守護，只是立體獅子的模樣則越來越渾圓可愛。獅子同樣隨著佛教傳到日本，日本神社則是由一種叫做狛犬的神獸守衛，就是由獅子演變而來。 The lion was known in antiquity as suanni, an Indian name that entered China with the spread of Buddhism. Although records note that lions were brought to China early on, they were extremely rare and even by the Ming dynasty were kept only in imperial menageries. Most artists never saw a real lion and had to rely instead on their imagination. In painting, lions are often portrayed at close to real scale, but the details diverge. What endures is an image of strength and majesty, which established the lion as a guardian of gates and entrances. In sculpture, however, lions gradually took more rounded, endearing forms. Buddhism took the lion motif to Japan, where Shinto shrines are now guarded by lion-like mythical beasts known as komainu.
